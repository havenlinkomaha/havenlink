About Haven Link

HavenLink is a free community resource website that helps people in Omaha find local support in one place. It connects users to information about shelters, meals, healthcare, legal help, transportation, supplies, and other free services. The goal is to make it easier for people in need to quickly find reliable resources when they need them most.

Who HavenLink Is For

HavenLink is for people experiencing homelessness, people at risk of losing housing, and anyone in Omaha who needs free food, healthcare, legal help, transportation support, or basic supplies.

How Listings Are Verified

Before a resource is added to HavenLink, we check it against information published by the organization itself. Listings are also reviewed regularly because hours, phone numbers, and services can change often.

Still, things can change between updates. When possible, please call ahead before visiting.

If you see something wrong or notice a missing resource, please tell us.

Who Is Behind HavenLink

HavenLink was founded and is run by Nandini Bansal, a student at Millard North High School in Omaha with a background in community health education.

It is an independent, volunteer-run community project.

How You Can Help

You can support HavenLink by suggesting a resource, reporting incorrect information, or reaching out if you represent an organization that wants to add or update a listing. HavenLink is also looking for students and volunteers who want to help verify listings, spread the word, translate information, or build new features. If you would like to contribute, email us and tell us how you would like to help.

Contact: havenlinkomaha@gmail.com

Please note: HavenLink is an independent community project, not a government service or a crisis line. Information can change without notice, so please call ahead when you can. For immediate help anywhere in Nebraska, dial 211. If this is an emergency, call 911.
